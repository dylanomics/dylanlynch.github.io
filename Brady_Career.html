Tom Brady’s Passing Statistics

Dylan Lynch

About Tom Brady

Tom Brady is a former American football quarterback who played in the National Football League (NFL) for 22 seasons. He spent his first 20 seasons with the New England Patriots, winning six Super Bowls, five Super Bowl MVPs, and three NFL MVPs. In his final two seasons, he was a member of the Tampa Bay Buccaneers where he won another Super Bowl and Super Bowl MVP.

His career highlights include: 7× Super Bowl champion (XXXVI, XXXVIII, XXXIX, XLIX, LI, LIII, LV), 5× Super Bowl MVP (XXXVI, XXXVIII, XLIX, LI, LV), 3× NFL Most Valuable Player (2007, 2010, 2017), 2× NFL Offensive Player of the Year (2007, 2010), NFL Comeback Player of the Year (2009), 3× First-team All-Pro (2007, 2010, 2017), 3× Second-team All-Pro (2005, 2016, 2021), 15× Pro Bowl (2001, 2004, 2005, 2007, 2009–2018, 2021), 5× NFL passing touchdowns leader (2002, 2007, 2010, 2015, 2021), 4× NFL passing yards leader (2005, 2007, 2017, 2021), and 2× NFL passer rating leader (2007, 2010).

Passing averages

Let’s see how many passing yards Tom Brady averaged in the regular and post season.

Regular season:

mean(Brady_Regular_Career$Yds) #Average regular season yards
## [1] 3841.818

Post season:

mean(Brady_Post_Career$Yds) #Average post season yards
## [1] 686.7895

Adjusted:

mean(Brady_Regular_Career_Adj$Yds) #Average adjusted regular season yards (dropping two seasons)
## [1] 4221.9

Why are there three sets of numbers? Brady_Regular_Career_Adj drops the 2000 and 2008 seasons. Why? In 2000, Tom Brady was 1/3 for six yards due to being a backup, actually starting the season as the fourth string QB. In 2008, Tom Brady was 7/11 for 76 yards due to a season ending knee injury in the first quarter of the first game of the season. In 2016, Tom Brady was suspended for the first four games of the season due to the “Deflategate scandal”. However, we will include this season because they ended up winning the Super Bowl that year. Also, no adjustments were made to his post season career.

Numbers are great, but visualization tells the story.

#Passing touchdowns
Reg_TD <- subset(Brady_Regular_Career, select = c(Age, TD))
Post_TD <- subset(Brady_Post_Career, select = c(Age, TD))
Combined_TD <- aggregate(TD ~ Age, data=subset(Brady_Combined, select = c(Age, TD)),sum)

#TD plot
TD_plot <- ggplot()+
  geom_line(Reg_TD, mapping = aes(Age, TD, color = "red"), lwd = 1)+
  geom_line(Post_TD, mapping = aes(Age, TD, color = "blue"), lwd = 1)+
  geom_line(Combined_TD, mapping = aes(Age, TD, color = "green"), lwd = 1)+
  theme_bw()+
  theme(panel.grid.major = element_blank(), panel.grid.minor = element_blank())+
  scale_color_identity(name = "",
                       breaks = c("red", "blue", "green"),
                       labels = c("Regular Season", "Post Season", "Combined"),
                       guide = "legend")+
  theme(legend.position="bottom")+
  ggtitle("Tom Brady Passing TDs per Age")
